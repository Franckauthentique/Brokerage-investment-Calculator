GDP — Economic Growth
The "Big Scoreboard"
GDP measures how much money a country made from all its activity — selling, building, working, buying. Think of it like a country's yearly salary.

When GDP is growing → the economy is healthy, companies make profits, people spend. When GDP shrinks → recession, jobs disappear, fear spreads.
👷
Labour Market — Jobs Data
The "Employment Health Check"
This tracks how many people have jobs and how many don't (unemployment rate). It also tracks wages — are workers earning more?

Low unemployment + rising wages = people have money to spend = good. High unemployment = people stop spending = economy slows. It's the heartbeat of consumer demand.
🔥
Inflation — Price Growth
The "Cost of Everything"
Inflation is how fast prices are rising. A little bit (around 2%) is healthy — it shows the economy is moving. Too much inflation = your money buys less every day.

High inflation is dangerous — it destroys purchasing power. Deflation (falling prices) is also dangerous — people stop buying because they wait for cheaper prices.
🏦
Monetary Policy — Central Banks
The "Money Tap" (Fed, ECB, BOE…)
Central banks (like the US Federal Reserve) control the price of money itself — interest rates. They also print or destroy money.

Raise rates = borrowing costs more → spending slows → inflation cools. Cut rates = borrowing is cheap → spending increases → economy heats up. It's their main lever to balance the economy.
🏛️
Fiscal Policy — Government
The "Spending & Tax" Lever
Governments can spend more (build roads, pay benefits, fund programs) or raise/cut taxes. This directly puts money into or takes it out of the economy.

Stimulus spending = more money in the economy = growth boost. Austerity = cutting spending = slows growth but reduces debt. Unlike central banks, this is slower — needs political approval.
⚡
How They're All Connected
The Triangle of Forces
GDP ↑ + Jobs ↑ → people earn and spend more → Inflation ↑
→ Central bank raises rates to cool it down → borrowing costs more → spending slows → GDP ↓

It's always a balancing act. Too hot = inflation crisis. Too cold = recession. The central bank targets the "Goldilocks zone" — strong growth with ~2% inflation.
